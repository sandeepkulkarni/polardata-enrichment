The Biogeographic Atlas of the Southern Ocean

\r\n\t \r\n\t\r\n\t
Background
\r\n\t

The Southern Ocean waters to the west of the Antarctic Peninsula are warming faster than almost any other place on Earth.\r\n\tThis area of most rapid environmental change was among others targeted by the Census of Antarctic Marine Life in its collection of biogeographic information.\r\n\tSuch biogeographic information is of fundamental importance for monitoring biodiversity, discovering biodiversity hotspots, defining ecoregions and detecting the impacts of environmental changes.\r\n\tIt is the preliminary and necessary step in designing marine protected areas in a changing ocean.\r\n\tAt the end of five years of extensive biodiversity exploration and assessment by CAML (www.caml.aq) and the OBIS Antarctic Node (the SCAR Marine Biyœodiversity Information Network, www.scarmarbin.be),\r\n\ta new initiative, the multi-authored \"CAML Biogeographic Atlas of the Southern Ocean\", has been established under the aegis of the Scientific Committee on Antarctic Research (SCAR)\r\n\tto provide an up-to-date synthesis of Antarctic and sub-Antarctic biogeographic knowledge and to make available a new comprehensive online resource for visualisation, analysis and modelling of species distribution.\r\n\tIt will constitute a major scientific output of CAML and SCAR-MarBIN as well as being a significant legacy of CoML and the International Polar Year to fulfill the needs of biogeographic information for science,\r\n\tconservation, monitoring and sustainable management of the changing Southern Ocean.\r\n\tIt will be of direct benefit to the Antarctic Treaty and associated bodies such as the Convention for the Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources.\r\n

\r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n \r\n\t

 

\r\n\t\r\n
Objectives
\r\n\t

Establish - on the basis of an unprecedented amount, diversity and quality of biogeographic and environmental data - a new synthesis of the biogeography of the Southern Ocean (patterns and processes),\r\n\tcovering benthos, zooplankton, nekton, birds and seals, to provide a benchmark of biogeographic knowledge at the end of CAML, and to help define the present biogeochemical and biogeographic provinces and\r\n\tto predict future changes under various climate change scenarios.
\r\n\tModel predictive distribution of species and assemblages according to abiotic factors.\r\n\tDevelop dynamic editions of the Atlas, including a dedicated webportal and an e-book, connected to a world network of interoperable databases, and offering advanced functionalities\r\n\tfor access to original occurrence and environmental data, customized mapping, data visualisation and analysis, to contribute to the ecoregionalisation process and the modelling of\r\n\tpredictive distribution of species and assemblages in the context of environmental changes.
